About Me

I'm a software engineer who builds user-centered applications from design to production. My background spans public health and software engineering, a combination that shapes how I think about products: who they serve, who they miss, and how to close that gap.

I started at Gallaudet University studying public health, then earned my M.S. in Software Engineering at UMGC. The pivot wasn't accidental because it came from watching technology either solve real problems or quietly create new ones for the people it was never designed with in mind.

Most recently I shipped DialTrend, a full-stack React web app from zero to live production in under two months.
