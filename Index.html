About Me

Code with
purpose.

I'm a software engineer who builds accessible, user-centered web applications from design to deployment. With a master's in software engineering and a background in public health, I bring a rare perspective: technology should serve everyone, not just those without barriers.

My path wasn't typical. I started at Gallaudet University studying public health, working to improve outcomes for underserved communities. When COVID-19 revealed how technology could either bridge or widen gaps in care, I pivoted to software engineering. Now I combine both worlds — technical depth and human empathy.

I'm currently building Yairo, a community platform that makes tech easier to use, while advising student teams on accessible UI implementation at UMGC.
